What are enterprise applications?

In the recent years, enterprise app development has become a norm to succeed in today’s competitive world of business. Enterprise or business mobile applications refer to mobile applications that are used in the world of business to solve the problems of enterprises. Enterprise app development is generally done with the intention of deployment to many different devices, networks and operating systems; but their main goal still includes - administrative management and security capabilities. Enterprise app development has seen an exponential rise with the rise in the number of employees using their own mobile devices for corporate applications and accessing company data. It is commonly referred to as - Bring Your Own Device (BYOD). By opting for enterprise app development, companies give their employees simplified access to the same applications as everyone else in the company. This creates uniformity between devices while still allowing employees to have the personalized mobile experience. Studies indicate that it has led to the benefit of increasing efficiency in the workplace, while cutting down the costs spent on company devices. Different types of apps are in trend these days for enterprise app development. For example:
  • Apps for content management
  • Apps for payment processing
  • Customer support apps
  • Automated billing systems
  • Systems for collaboration and messaging
  • Email marketing systems
  • Customer relationship management (CRM)
  • Enterprise application integration (EAI)
  • Business Intelligence apps
Enterprise mobile applications are also referred to as enterprise mobile application software, or simply enterprise mobile softwares.

Challenges faced in enterprise app development:

  • Security: With employees using their personal mobile devices to access company data, security is a major concern in enterprise app development.
  • Accessibility: This is another challenging area in enterprise app development. This helps organizations make fast and informed decisions.
  • Backup of data: It is important to ensure backup of the data to ensure consistent performance across the enterprise.
  • Seamless sharing of data: There are challenges of seamless sharing of data. Seamless sharing of data should be allowed inside and outside the firewall access.
  • Making room for both current and future requirements of enterprises: During enterprise app development, not just the current technology but the evolving technology has to be taken into consideration too, which can be difficult as the technologies keep evolving.
  • Finding the right skills: Enterprise app development is different from the regular app development. It involves integration of systems, which makes it slightly more complex. Finding the right skills to match the needs of enterprise app development is another challenge that companies face.
